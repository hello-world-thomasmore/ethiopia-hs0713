Ethiopia Dried Legumes Sourcing Pack

Ethiopia Legumes Export Desk • Trade Support Team This pack is for buyers of chickpeas and lentils under HS 0713 (the customs code for dried leguminous vegetables, shelled).
What you get on this page: why Ethiopia is a reliable origin, five qualified suppliers, how we check quality, and how you can start buying within one week. Goal: stable cost and stable delivery.
Why Ethiopia for HS 0713
Cost • Capacity • Compliance • Logistics
Cost position
Margin control
Competitive farm cost and efficient cleaning/packing mean attractive landed cost without dropping quality. This protects your margin instead of letting price swings eat it.
Capacity
Seasonal volume
Chickpeas and lentils are grown at scale in organized regions such as Amhara and Oromia. This supports repeat volume, not just one-off loads.
Compliance
Food-safety docs
Processors provide food-safety documentation and follow moisture / size / cleanliness specs for export. You are not buying blind.
Logistics
Known route
Standard corridor: Ethiopia → Port of Djibouti → vessel. This is the normal export route for legumes. Transit is known. Timing is managed.

Quality Check: Incoming Sample
Chickpeas / Lentils
  • Moisture is inside the agreed range for that crop.
  • Size / calibration is even. No big mix of grain sizes.
  • Clean: no stones, no metal, no other foreign material in the 25 kg test bag.
  • Cook test: normal texture and taste after cooking, no off-flavor.
  • Bag weight is correct, bag is sealed, and date code is present.
This is the same first check we ask buyers to run on the sample bags: 1 x 25 kg chickpeas, 1 x 25 kg lentils.
How to Start (≈1 Week)
Step 1 • Step 2 • Step 3
1
Shortlist suppliers
We connect you with five Ethiopian suppliers that already meet food-safety requirements and already export. You speak to real processors, not unverified contacts.
2
Run samples
You receive one 25 kg bag of chickpeas and one 25 kg bag of lentils. You check moisture, size, cleanliness, and cooking performance. This takes about a week.
3
First order & ship
If the samples pass, you place your first order. We arrange container load on the standard Ethiopia → Port of Djibouti → vessel route. This is the normal export path for legumes, used daily.
Result: you are not “testing Ethiopia.” You are already buying from Ethiopia with defined specs, stable cost, and a known logistics path.
